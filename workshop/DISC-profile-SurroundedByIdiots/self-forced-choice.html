Forced-choice self-test

The classic DISC approach some practitioners prefer: for each group of four words, pick the one that is most like you and the one that is least like you. Compare your result with the Likert test to see which feels truer.

Your dominant style (forced-choice)

Comparing methods: forced-choice (ipsative) scoring exaggerates your strongest and weakest colours because every pick trades one colour off against another. The Likert test lets colours score independently, so mild blends show up more softly. Neither is “correct” — together they triangulate the real you.
